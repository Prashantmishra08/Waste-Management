One solution for disposing of biodegradable waste is composting. This involves collecting organic waste such as food scraps and yard trimmings and allowing them to decompose into nutrient-rich soil. Composting can be done on a small scale in a backyard or on a larger scale in a community composting facility. By composting, we can divert waste from landfills and create a valuable resource for gardening and farming.

Recycling is a great solution for disposing of recyclable waste. Instead of throwing away materials like paper, plastic, glass, and metal, we can recycle them to create new products. This reduces the amount of waste that ends up in landfills and helps conserve natural resources. To make recycling easier, you can separate your recyclable materials from other types of waste and place them in designated recycling bins. Many cities and towns also have recycling programs that provide curbside pickup or drop-off locations for recyclable materials. By recycling, we can all do our part to help protect the environment .
